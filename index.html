Frequently asked questions

GEO is the practice of optimising your website so it is cited, quoted and recommended by AI-powered search engines such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews and Claude. Unlike traditional SEO (which targets ten blue links), GEO focuses on the signals that large language models use when deciding which sources to trust and reference in their answers.
Completely free. You enter a URL, we analyse it, and you get your overall GEO score out of 100 with your tier label. No email required, no credit card, no trial period. The free score is permanently free. We make our money when you want the full category breakdown and actionable fix list - that's the paid report.
We audit six categories - AI Citability, Brand Authority, Content Quality and E-E-A-T, Technical Foundations, Structured Data, and Platform Optimisation. Each category scores 0-100 and carries a different weight in the final score (25%, 20%, 20%, 15%, 10%, 10%). We check over 50 individual signals including llms.txt presence, robots.txt AI bot rules, schema markup, OpenGraph tags, author bios and Wikipedia presence.
Most audits complete in under 30 seconds. Complex sites with many redirects or slow servers may take up to 60 seconds. We cache results for 24 hours so if you run the same URL twice in a day the second result is instant.
Traditional SEO targets ranking positions in keyword-based search results. GEO targets inclusion in AI-generated answers. The signals overlap but are not identical - for example, GEO places heavy emphasis on structured data schema, explicit AI bot permissions, llms.txt files, E-E-A-T author signals and Wikipedia presence, which matter far less in traditional SEO.
Yes. SearchScore audits any publicly accessible website - B2B SaaS, ecommerce, publishers, service businesses, agencies, blogs. The scoring categories are weighted to reflect the signals that matter for AI citability regardless of industry. If your site is behind a login wall or blocks all bots, some checks will score lower by default.
